Title: 163MO First-in-human phase I study of the anti-TIGIT and PVRIG bispecific antibody as monotherapy or with sintilimab for advanced solid tumors

URL Source: https://www.annalsofoncology.org/article/S0923-7534(25)05949-6/fulltext

Markdown Content:
## Background

This phase 1 first-in-human study investigates safety and efficacy of SIM0348, an anti-TIGIT/PVRIG (T cell Immunoglobulin and ITIM domains/Poliovirus receptor-related immunoglobin domain-containing protein) bispecific antibody, as a single agent or in combination with sintilimab in advanced solid tumors.

## Methods

The study was designed as a dose escalation and dose expansion part (Part 1) followed by a cohort expansion part (Part 2). The data from Part 1 is reported in this abstract. Part 1A was to evaluate single agent SIM0348 at the dose ranges of 10mg to 100mg iv once weekly or 100mg to 1200mg iv once every 3 weeks. Two dose levels (600mg and 1200mg iv once every 3 weeks) were evaluated in Part 1B when they were combined with Sintilimab . For Part 1, primary objective was to determine the maximum-tolerated dose (MTD) or recommended dose (RD). Secondary objectives were to assess preliminary anti-tumor activity using RECISIT v1.1, pharmacokinetics and immunogenicity. Exploratory objective was to evaluate pharmacodynamics.

## Results

As of April 30,2025, 49 participants were enrolled (25 monotherapy; 24 combination therapy). Dose-limiting toxicity (DLT) (Grade 4 Hypercalcemia)was reported in 1 participant who received monotherapy, but none in combination therapy, MTD was not reached in monotherapy and combination therapy. Treatment related adverse events (TRAEs) were reported in 96.0% (20.0% in ≥ Grade 3) of participants receiving monotherapy. TRAEs were reported in 79.2% (12.5% in≥Grade 3) receiving combination therapy. ORR was 0% with monotherapy and 8.3%(2/24) with combination in solid tumors. Specifically, 18 participants with heavily treated NSCLC in combination therapy, ORR was 11.1%(2/18). The pharmacokinetic data showed that the exposure was increased proportionally with the doses. The half-life of SIM0348 was 6.70-8.29 days after the first dose administration. The accumulation of PK exposure after multiple doses is limited with the ratio to the first dose less than 2. Dose of ≥ 30 mg QW or ≥ 100 mg Q3W archived almost complete TIGIT and PVRIG RO in peripheral T cells.

## Conclusions

SIM0348 plus sintilimab was well tolerated and demonstrated antitumor activity in participants with advanced NSCLC.
